About Me

I am an environmental researcher with a background in Microbiology (B.Sc.) and Environmental Science (M.sc) from Tongji University, China. My research has focused on nutrient and antibiotic removal in biofilm reactors for high-ammonium wastewater, exploring ecological and bioremediation strategies to combat pollution and climate change.

I'm particularly interested in sustainable solutions and biological nutrient recovery, and I aim to apply innovative science to real-world environmental and societal challenges.

I am a highly adaptable and service-driven professional with a strong academic and practical background in environmental science, research, and organizational support. With experience spanning administrative roles, quality control, and community health outreach, I bring excellent analytical thinking, communication, and team coordination skills to the workplace.

My leadership in cross-functional environments, from education to health and youth development, demonstrates my ability to manage tasks efficiently, foster collaboration, and support community-centered goals. I am passionate about contributing to people-focused sectors, where I can apply my diverse experiences to support inclusive growth, operational efficiency, and positive social impact.
